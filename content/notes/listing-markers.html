This page compares common Japanese forms used to list items, give examples, or show inclusion across multiple listed items. These forms mainly connect nouns, noun phrases, or rough examples rather than linking full clauses by reason or contrast.

Form Connects Scenario Examples Notes
〜と Noun Exhaustive listing
パンと卵を買った。
I bought bread and eggs.
 Presents the listed items as a closed set.
〜や Noun Non-exhaustive listing
パンや卵を買った。
I bought things like bread and eggs.
 Gives representative examples rather than a complete list.
〜とか〜とか Noun / phrase Casual rough listing
休みの日は、映画とかゲームとかをする。
On my days off, I do things like watch movies and play games.
 Casual and loose. Often used in speech to give rough examples.
〜も〜も Noun Both A and B
コーヒーも紅茶も飲む。
I drink both coffee and tea.
 Emphasizes inclusion of all listed items.
