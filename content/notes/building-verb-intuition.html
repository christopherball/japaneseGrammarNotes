Transitive Verbs

Form Verb Intuitive Phrase Example
Plain
食べる
someone eats something
子どもがりんごを食べる。
The child eats an apple.
褒める
someone praises someone
先生が学生を褒める。
The teacher praises the student.
頼む
someone asks someone for something
私が友達に助けを頼む。
I ask my friend for help.
Negative
食べる
someone does not eat something
子どもが野菜を食べない。
The child does not eat vegetables.
褒める
someone does not praise someone
先生がその学生を褒めない。
The teacher does not praise that student.
頼む
someone does not ask someone for something
私は彼に何も頼まない。
I do not ask him for anything.
Past
食べる
someone ate something
子どもがりんごを食べた。
The child ate an apple.
褒める
someone praised someone
先生が学生を褒めた。
The teacher praised the student.
頼む
someone asked someone for something
私が友達に助けを頼んだ。
I asked my friend for help.
Passive
食べる
something is eaten by someone
りんごが子どもに食べられた。
The apple was eaten by the child.
褒める
someone is praised by someone
学生が先生に褒められた。
The student was praised by the teacher.
頼む
someone is asked for something by someone
私が友達に手伝いを頼まれた。
My friend asked me for help.
Causative
食べる
someone makes someone eat something
母が子どもに野菜を食べさせる。
The mother makes the child eat vegetables.
褒める
someone makes someone praise someone
校長が先生に学生を褒めさせる。
The principal makes the teacher praise the student.
頼む
someone makes someone ask someone for something
上司が私に部長に許可を頼ませる。
My boss makes me ask the department head for permission.
Causative-passive
食べる
someone is made to eat something
子どもが母に野菜を食べさせられる。
The child is made by the mother to eat vegetables.
褒める
someone is made to praise someone
先生が校長に学生を褒めさせられる。
The teacher is made by the principal to praise the student.
頼む
someone is made to ask someone for something
私が上司に部長に許可を頼ませられる。
I am made by my boss to ask the department head for permission.
Potential
食べる
someone can eat something
子どもはそのりんごを食べられる。
The child can eat that apple.
褒める
someone can praise someone
先生はその学生を褒められる。
The teacher can praise that student.
頼む
someone can ask someone for something
私は彼に協力を頼める。
I can ask him for his cooperation.
Volitional
食べる
someone will eat something
私はこのケーキを食べよう。
I'll eat this cake.
褒める
someone will praise someone
先生は次もその学生を褒めよう。
The teacher will praise that student again next time.
頼む
someone will ask someone for something
私は先生に推薦状を頼もう。
I'll ask the teacher for a letter of recommendation.
たい
食べる
someone wants to eat something
私はそのケーキを食べたい。
I want to eat that cake.
褒める
someone wants to praise someone
私は彼を褒めたい。
I want to praise him.
頼む
someone wants to ask someone for something
私は友達に助けを頼みたい。
I want to ask my friend for help.
ている
食べる
someone is eating something
彼は朝ご飯を食べている。
He is eating breakfast.
褒める
someone is praising someone
先生がみんなの前で学生を褒めている。
The teacher is praising the student in front of everyone.
頼む
someone is asking someone for something
彼は今、友達に助けを頼んでいる。
He's asking his friend for help right now.
てしまう
食べる
someone ends up eating something
子どもがお菓子を全部食べてしまった。
The child ended up eating all the snacks.
褒める
someone ends up praising someone
先生は思わず彼を褒めてしまった。
The teacher ended up praising him without meaning to.
頼む
someone ends up asking someone for something
私はつい彼にお金を頼んでしまった。
I ended up asking him for money.

Intransitive Verbs

Form Verb Intuitive Phrase Example
Plain
行く
someone goes
彼が学校に行く。
He goes to school.
起きる
someone wakes up
彼が六時に起きる。
He wakes up at six.
座る
someone sits
彼がいすに座る。
He sits in a chair.
Negative
行く
someone does not go
彼は学校に行かない。
He does not go to school.
起きる
someone does not wake up
彼はなかなか起きない。
He does not wake up easily.
座る
someone does not sit
彼はいすに座らない。
He does not sit in the chair.
Past
行く
someone went
彼が学校に行った。
He went to school.
起きる
someone woke up
彼が六時に起きた。
He woke up at six.
座る
someone sat
彼がいすに座った。
He sat in a chair.
Passive
行く
someone is affected by someone going
私は友達に先に行かれた。
My friend went on ahead of me, leaving me behind.
起きる
someone is affected by someone waking up
私は子どもに早く起きられた。
The child woke up early, and it affected me.
座る
someone is affected by someone sitting
私は隣の人に近くに座られた。
The person next to me sat too close to me.
Causative
行く
someone makes someone go
母が子どもを学校に行かせる。
The mother makes the child go to school.
起きる
someone makes someone wake up
母が子どもを早く起きさせる。
The mother makes the child wake up early.
座る
someone makes someone sit
先生が学生をいすに座らせる。
The teacher makes the student sit in a chair.
Causative-passive
行く
someone is made to go
子どもが母に学校に行かせられる。
The child is made by the mother to go to school.
起きる
someone is made to wake up
子どもが母に早く起きさせられる。
The child is made by the mother to wake up early.
座る
someone is made to sit
学生が先生にいすに座らせられる。
The student is made by the teacher to sit in a chair.
Potential
行く
someone can go
私は明日学校に行ける。
I can go to school tomorrow.
起きる
someone can wake up
彼は六時に起きられる。
He can wake up at six.
座る
someone can sit
私はそこに座れる。
I can sit there.
Volitional
行く
someone will go
私は明日そこに行こう。
I'll go there tomorrow.
起きる
someone will wake up
明日は六時に起きよう。
I'll wake up at six tomorrow.
座る
someone will sit
私はここに座ろう。
I'll sit here.
たい
行く
someone wants to go
私は日本に行きたい。
I want to go to Japan.
起きる
someone wants to wake up
明日は早く起きたい。
I want to wake up early tomorrow.
座る
someone wants to sit
私はそこに座りたい。
I want to sit there.
ている
行く
someone is going
彼は今、学校に行っている。
He's on his way to school now.
起きる
someone is awake
彼はもう起きている。
He's already awake.
座る
someone is sitting
彼は窓のそばに座っている。
He is sitting by the window.
てしまう
行く
someone ends up going
彼は一人で行ってしまった。
He ended up going by himself.
起きる
someone ends up waking up
彼は朝早く起きてしまった。
He ended up waking up early in the morning.
座る
someone ends up sitting
私は後ろの席に座ってしまった。
I ended up sitting in the back.
