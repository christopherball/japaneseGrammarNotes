This table collects common V2 verbs in V1てV2 constructions and shows how each one reshapes the meaning of the original V1 action.

V2 verb Precise meaning relative to X (V1) Examples
Core aspectual verbs
みる
Try X; do X and see what happens
Implies tentative attempt, not visual seeing
新しいアプリを使ってみた。
I tried using a new app.
少し考えてみます。
I'll try thinking about it a little.
食べてみてください。
Please give it a try (and taste it).
おく
Do X in advance; leave things in a state where X is already handled
Forward-looking preparation; the effect persists
先に予約しておいた。
I reserved it in advance.
必要なところをメモしておく。
I'll jot down the necessary parts (for later).
ドアを開けておいて。
Leave the door open (for a reason).
しまう
X ends up happening; often regrettably, accidentally, or beyond one's control. It can also simply mean "finish X completely."
財布をなくしてしまった。
I ended up losing my wallet. (regret)
宿題をやってしまった。
I got the homework done (and out of the way).
食べてしまった…
I ate it all… (couldn't help myself)
いく
X proceeds away from the present point onward; change or development continues into the future
Direction: now → future
日本語が分かっていく。
I gradually come to understand Japanese (from now on).
気温が下がっていく。
The temperature keeps dropping.
社会は変わっていくだろう。
Society will keep changing.
くる
X comes about up to the present point; a change has been developing and has arrived at now
Direction: past → now (contrast with いく)
雨が降ってきた。
It has started raining (and is now raining).
日本語が分かってきた。
I've been starting to understand Japanese.
疲れてきた。
I've been getting tired.
ある
X has been done intentionally and the resulting state persists; the effect was deliberately set up
vs. ている: ある stresses intent behind the result
会場はきれいに片づけてある。
The venue has been tidied (and is ready).
ドアが閉めてある。
The door has been left closed (on purpose).
名前が書いてある。
A name has been written (there).
いる
X is ongoing right now, or the state resulting from X holds currently
vs. てある: いる is neutral about intent
彼は座っている。
He is sitting. (ongoing action)
窓が開いている。
The window is open. (resulting state)
雨が降っている。
It is raining.
だす
X suddenly begins; an action bursts into motion, often unexpectedly
子供が急に泣き出した。
The child suddenly burst into tears.
雨が降り出した。
It suddenly started raining.
彼女は走り出した。
She broke into a run.
おわる
X is finished; an action reaches its natural end point
レポートを書き終わった。
I finished writing the report.
食べ終わったら教えて。
Let me know when you're done eating.
映画を見終わった。
I finished watching the movie.
Giving & receiving verbs (やりもらい)
あげる
Do X for someone else (speaker or in-group → other); a favor directed outward
Not used when recipient is the speaker
彼に教えてあげた。
I taught him (as a favor).
荷物を持ってあげる。
I'll carry the bags for her.
子供に読んであげた。
I read to the child.
くれる
Someone does X for me, us, or my in-group; a favor directed inward toward the speaker
Subject is always someone outside the speaker
友達が教えてくれた。
A friend explained it to me.
母がケーキを作ってくれた。
My mother made me a cake.
先生が助けてくれた。
The teacher helped me out.
くださる
Someone socially above the speaker does X for me or us; honorific form of くれる
Use toward superiors, teachers, or customers
先生が説明してくださった。
The teacher kindly explained it to me.
部長が手伝ってくださいます。
The manager is kindly helping me.
ご確認してください。
Please (kindly) confirm. (request form)
もらう
Receive the benefit of someone doing X; have someone do X from the speaker's receiving perspective
友達に手伝ってもらった。
I had a friend help me.
先生に説明してもらう。
I'll have the teacher explain it.
医者に診てもらった。
I had the doctor examine me.
Volition & desire
みせる
Do X demonstratively to prove oneself; strong personal resolve expressed through action
"I'll show you (that I can do it)"
絶対に勝ってみせる。
I'll definitely win; just watch me.
やり遂げてみせる。
I'll see it through, no matter what.
一人でできるってみせたい。
I want to show that I can do it alone.
ほしい
Want someone else to do X; expresses the speaker's desire for another person's action
Subject of X must be someone other than the speaker
もっと話してほしい。
I want you to talk more.
早く来てほしかった。
I wanted you to come sooner.
彼に謝ってほしい。
I want him to apologize.
