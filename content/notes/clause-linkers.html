Connector Core Meaning Read Clause 1 As... Typical Nuance Examples
から because / since the direct reason for what follows direct, speaker-assertive reason
雨が降ったから、試合は中止になった。
Because it rained, the game was canceled.
頭が痛いから、早く帰ります。
Because I have a headache, I'll go home early.
ので because / since the explanatory background reason softer, more neutral, less blunt than から
電車が遅れたので、遅刻しました。
Because the train was late, I got there late.
危ないので、入らないでください。
Because it's dangerous, please don't go in.
けど but / though a fact that contrasts with or limits what follows common in speech; often softens what comes next
行きたいけど、時間がない。
I want to go, but I don't have time.
この店は高いけど、おいしい。
This restaurant is expensive, but it's delicious.
が but / though a fact that contrasts with what follows, or a polite lead-in to what follows contrast in statements; often a polite transition in requests or softening expressions
行きたいですが、今日は無理です。
I want to go, but it won't work today.
すみませんが、もう一度お願いします。
Excuse me, but could you do that one more time?
のに even though / despite that background that should have led to a different result contrary expectation; often surprise, frustration, or complaint
勉強したのに、失敗した。
Even though I studied, I failed.
もう春なのに、まだ寒い。
Even though it's already spring, it's still cold.
と when / if the trigger that automatically leads to the next clause automatic, regular, or natural consequence; usually not used when clause 2 is a decision, intention, request, invitation, or command
春になると、花が咲く。
When spring comes, flowers bloom.
このボタンを押すと、ドアが開きます。
When you press this button, the door opens.
たら if / when / after the event that must happen first once X happens, then Y; event-first and very flexible in real use
雨がやんだら、出かけます。
If the rain stops, I'll go out.
家に帰ったら、電話してください。
When you get home, please call me.
ば if the condition under which clause 2 holds conditional or hypothetical relationship; often less tied to a concrete event sequence than たら
時間があれば、行きます。
If I have time, I'll go.
安ければ、買います。
If it's cheap, I'll buy it.
なら if / given that the assumption, topic, or case now being considered reactive, assumption-based, often used when responding to what has just been said or when narrowing to a specific case
日本に行くなら、京都にも行きたい。
If I'm going to Japan, I want to go to Kyoto too.
時間がないなら、今日はやめましょう。
If we don't have time, let's stop for today.
ても / でも even if / even though a condition that still does not change the result concessive: clause 2 holds even if clause 1 is true
雨が降っても、行きます。
Even if it rains, I'll go.
高くても、必要なら買います。
Even if it's expensive, I'll buy it if I need it.
