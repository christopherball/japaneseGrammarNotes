This page covers sentence-level connectors that link one sentence or thought to the next. Unlike forms such as 〜て or から, these usually sit at the beginning of a new sentence and shape the flow of the discourse.

Connector Core Meaning Use It For... Typical Nuance Examples
Additive / sequence
そして and; and then adding the next event or point in a neutral written/spoken style simple progression or addition; not the normal choice for cause/result
朝ご飯を食べた。そして、学校に行った。
I ate breakfast. And then I went to school.
この本は分かりやすい。そして、例文も多い。
This book is easy to understand. It also has a lot of example sentences.
それから after that; and then moving step by step through a sequence of events temporal progression is especially clear
宿題をした。それから、映画を見た。
I did my homework. After that, I watched a movie.
駅で友だちに会った。それから、一緒に昼ご飯を食べた。
I met my friend at the station. After that, we ate lunch together.
それに besides; moreover adding another supporting reason or point often feels like “and on top of that”
この店は安い。それに、おいしい。
This restaurant is cheap. On top of that, it's tasty.
彼は親切だ。それに、説明も上手だ。
He's kind. On top of that, he's also good at explaining things.
また also; additionally adding another point in neutral or formal explanation common in writing, presentations, and explanations
この文法は会話でよく使う。また、作文でも役に立つ。
This grammar is often used in conversation. It's also useful in writing.
この駅は便利だ。また、周りに店も多い。
This station is convenient. There are also a lot of shops nearby.
さらに furthermore intensifying or extending a previous point stronger than simple “also”; often used in structured explanation
彼は日本語が話せる。さらに、中国語も話せる。
He can speak Japanese. Furthermore, he can speak Chinese as well.
この薬は安い。さらに、効果も早い。
This medicine is inexpensive. Furthermore, it works quickly.
そのうえ on top of that; moreover stacking an additional point with a stronger cumulative feel often emphasizes that the second point makes the overall case even stronger
彼は経験がある。そのうえ、教えるのも上手だ。
He has experience. On top of that, he's also good at teaching.
このホテルは駅に近い。そのうえ、値段も安い。
This hotel is close to the station. On top of that, it's affordable.
Result / conclusion
だから so; therefore stating a direct conclusion from the previous sentence common in speech; straightforward and speaker-forward
今日は日曜日だ。だから、学校は休みだ。
Today is Sunday. So there's no school.
雨が降っている。だから、出かけない。
It's raining. So I'm not going out.
それで and so; because of that showing that the second sentence follows as a result often sounds like natural narrative consequence
電車が遅れた。それで、会議に遅れた。
The train was delayed. So I was late for the meeting.
昨日はあまり寝なかった。それで、今日は眠い。
I didn't sleep much yesterday. Because of that, I'm sleepy today.
そのため for that reason; therefore giving a more formal result or consequence common in written explanation, reports, and careful exposition
台風が近づいている。そのため、試合は中止になった。
A typhoon is approaching. For that reason, the game was canceled.
材料が不足している。そのため、生産が遅れている。
Materials are lacking. Therefore, production is delayed.
したがって accordingly; therefore drawing a formal or logical conclusion more formal and deductive than だから
この条件は満たされていない。したがって、申請は認められない。
These conditions are not satisfied. Accordingly, the application cannot be approved.
証拠が十分ではない。したがって、結論は保留する。
The evidence is not sufficient. Therefore, we will reserve judgment.
Contrast / qualification
でも but; however introducing an everyday contrast or objection very common in conversation; casual and direct
行きたい。でも、時間がない。
I want to go. But I don't have time.
この店は高い。でも、おいしい。
This restaurant is expensive. But it's tasty.
しかし however introducing a firmer contrast in neutral or formal style stronger and more written-sounding than でも
計画は良かった。しかし、実行が難しかった。
The plan was good. However, carrying it out was difficult.
彼は努力した。しかし、結果は出なかった。
He made an effort. However, it didn't pay off.
ところが however; but then marking an unexpected turn against expectation often carries surprise: “but contrary to what you’d expect...”
簡単だと思った。ところが、全然できなかった。
I thought it would be easy. However, I couldn't do it at all.
天気予報では晴れだった。ところが、午後から雨が降った。
The forecast said it would be sunny. However, it started raining in the afternoon.
ただし however; provided that adding a limitation, condition, or exception to what was just said common in instructions, rules, and formal explanation
この図書館は誰でも使えます。ただし、貸し出しには登録が必要です。
Anyone can use this library. However, borrowing books requires registration.
参加は自由です。ただし、事前に連絡してください。
Participation is open. That said, please contact us in advance.
