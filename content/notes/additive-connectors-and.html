This page compares common Japanese forms that add, list, and link information: clause connectors like 〜て and 〜し, noun linkers like と and や, and adjective / noun linkers such as 〜で and 〜くて.

Form Connects Scenario Examples Notes
〜て Clause (verb) Sequence
朝ご飯を食べて、学校に行く。
Eat breakfast and go to school.
 Default clause linker. Interpreted as sequence or simple addition depending on context.
〜て Clause (verb) Cause/result (soft)
雨が降って、試合が中止になった。
It rained, and the game was canceled.
 Can imply causality, but this is a tendency, not encoded explicitly.
〜て Clause (verb) State chaining
ドアが開いて、風が入ってきた。
The door opened and wind came in.
 Often links events that feel naturally connected in time or situation.
〜て Clause (verb) Ongoing -> result
音楽を聞いていて、寝てしまった。
I was listening to music and fell asleep.
 First clause often provides background state.
〜し Clause (predicate) Listing qualities
彼は優しいし、頭もいい。
He's kind and smart.
 Enumerates separate points. Each clause stands independently.
〜し Clause (predicate) Multiple reasons
雨だし、時間もないし、行かない。
It's raining and I don't have time, so I won't go.
 Commonly used for justification; can imply "so."
〜し Clause (predicate) Open-ended listing
安いし、おいしい。
It's cheap and tasty (among other things).
 Often implies additional unstated reasons.
〜し Clause (predicate) Additive (written)
次の表現を勉強し、文も作る。
Study the following expressions and also make sentences.
 In written style, can resemble 〜て but still retains a "listing" feel.
〜と Noun Exhaustive listing
パンと卵を買った。
Bought bread and eggs.
 Complete list (closed set).
〜や Noun Non-exhaustive listing
パンや卵を買った。
Bought things like bread and eggs.
 Indicates examples, not a full list.
〜とか〜とか Noun / clause Casual listing
映画とかゲームとかする。
Do things like movies and games.
 Casual, often used in speech; similar to 〜や but looser.
〜も〜も Noun Both A and B
コーヒーも紅茶も飲む。
Drink both coffee and tea.
 Emphasizes inclusion of all listed items.
〜で Noun / な-adj State linking
彼は学生で、アルバイトもしている。
He's a student and also works part-time.
 Links states/roles; second clause often adds new info about same subject.
〜で Noun / な-adj Reason nuance (additive)
病気で、学校を休んだ。
I was sick and missed school.
 Frequently read as cause; additive reading still possible.
〜くて い-adj State linking
安くておいしい。
Cheap and delicious.
 Merges properties into a single combined description.
〜くて い-adj Cause nuance (additive)
頭が痛くて、寝た。
My head hurt and I went to sleep.
 Often implies cause, but weaker than explicit causal forms.
