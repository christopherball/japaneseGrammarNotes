Particle First parse while reading Also check for Key contrast / note Examples
が the noun most directly tied to the predicate; often the subject with perception/psychology predicates, may mark the thing perceived, understood, liked, etc. rather than the experiencer Do not confuse with は. が identifies the noun the predicate applies to most directly; は sets the topic/contrast frame. 猫がいる
there is a cat

雨が降る
rain falls

日本語が分かる
understand Japanese

猫が好きだ
like cats
を direct object; the thing directly affected by the action with movement/traversal predicates, may mark the path crossed or the place exited When the verb suggests motion, ask whether を is really an object. ご飯を食べる
eat rice

本を読む
read a book

公園を歩く
walk through the park

家を出る
leave the house
に destination, target, recipient, time, location of existence with change-of-state predicates, may mark the resulting state A useful contrast: に often marks where something ends up / is aimed / exists. 学校に行く
go to school

友だちに会う
meet a friend

七時に起きる
wake up at 7

机の上に本がある
there is a book on the desk

医者になる
become a doctor
へ direction toward Usually similar to に with motion, but more “toward” than “arrive at.” 東京へ行く
go to Tokyo / head to Tokyo

南へ向かう
head south
で location where an action happens; means / instrument A useful contrast: で marks where an action is carried out; に marks where something exists / ends up. 駅で待つ
wait at the station

公園で遊ぶ
play in the park

バスで行く
go by bus

ナイフで切る
cut with a knife
と with; and; quotation; counterpart と lists exhaustively; や is non-exhaustive. It also marks quoted words/thoughts. 友だちと行く
go with a friend

本とノート
books and notebooks

「大丈夫」と言った
said “It’s okay”
から from; starting point; source reason / cause (“because”) Often pairs with まで for “from X to Y.” 東京から来た
came from Tokyo

九時から始まる
starts at 9

寒いから帰る
go home because it’s cold
まで until; as far as; endpoint / limit Often pairs with から. 金曜日まで働く
work until Friday

駅まで歩く
walk as far as the station
より than; comparison basis source in formal/literary contexts In everyday comparison, it often appears with ほうが. 電車より速い
faster than the train

昨日より忙しい
busier than yesterday
も also; too can replace a case particle or stack after one Read it first as “also / too,” then check what it is attaching to. 私も行く
I’m going too

猫も好きだ
I also like cats

家にもある
it is also at home / there is one at home too
は topic; contrast は is not the same as が. It frames what the sentence is about, and often implies contrast even if subtle. 私は学生だ
as for me, I’m a student

魚は食べる
I eat fish / fish, I do eat
の possession; attribution; noun-to-noun linking clause nominalization in some contexts Read it first as “X modifies Y.” Sometimes that is possession, sometimes category/attribute. 先生の本
the teacher’s book

日本の会社
a Japanese company

赤いのが欲しい
I want the red one

食べるのが好きだ
like eating
や non-exhaustive listing: “X, Y, and such” Unlike と, it suggests other items are not being listed. 本や雑誌
books, magazines, and such

りんごやバナナを買う
buy apples, bananas, and so on
か question marker; alternative embedded question / uncertainty At sentence end, it often marks a question. Inside a clause, it often means “whether / which / where / etc.” 行きますか
are you going?

コーヒーかお茶
coffee or tea

どこへ行くか分からない
I don’t know where [someone] is going
