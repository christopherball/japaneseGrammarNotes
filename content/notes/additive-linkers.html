Form Connects Scenario Examples Notes
〜て Clause (verb) Sequence
朝ご飯を食べて、学校に行く。
I eat breakfast and then go to school.
 Default clause linker. Interpreted as sequence or simple addition depending on context.
〜て Clause (verb) Cause/result (soft)
雨が降って、試合が中止になった。
It rained, and the game was canceled.
 Can imply causality, but this is a tendency, not encoded explicitly.
〜て Clause (verb) State chaining
ドアが開いて、風が入ってきた。
The door opened, and the wind came in.
 Often links events that feel naturally connected in time or situation.
〜て Clause (verb) Ongoing -> result
音楽を聞いていて、寝てしまった。
I was listening to music and fell asleep.
 First clause often provides background state.
〜し Clause (predicate) Listing qualities
彼は優しいし、頭もいい。
He's kind and smart.
 Enumerates separate points. Each clause stands independently.
〜し Clause (predicate) Multiple reasons
雨だし、時間もないし、行かない。
It's raining and I don't have time, so I won't go.
 Commonly used for justification; can imply "so."
〜し Clause (predicate) Open-ended listing
安いし、おいしい。
It's cheap and tasty (among other things).
 Often implies additional unstated reasons.
〜し Clause (predicate) Additive (written)
次の表現を勉強し、文も作る。
Study the following expressions and write some sentences too.
 In written style, can resemble 〜て but still retains a "listing" feel.
〜で Noun / な-adj State linking
彼は学生で、アルバイトもしている。
He's a student, and he also works part-time.
 Links states, roles, or descriptions related to the same subject.
〜で Noun / な-adj Reason / cause
病気で、学校を休んだ。
I missed school because I was sick.
 Often gives a straightforward reason, especially with conditions, situations, or circumstances.
〜くて い-adj State linking
安くておいしい。
It's cheap and delicious.
 Links qualities into a combined description.
〜くて い-adj Reason / cause
頭が痛くて、寝た。
I went to bed because my head hurt.
 Often gives a natural reason, especially with feelings, physical states, or conditions.
