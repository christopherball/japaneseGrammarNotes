This page treats the Form column as the pivot: read left for representative 五段 (ごだん) patterns and right for matching 一段 (いちだん) patterns. The highlighted suffix pieces mark the endings being attached or swapped in each conjugation.

How to read the main table: the left half shows what happens to a representative godan verb like 書く, the center names the form, and the right half shows the matching ichidan pattern with 食べる. The far-left あ / い / う / え / お row labels apply to the godan side only.

て-Form (+ た-Form) Conjugation

For godan verbs, the て-form and た-form depend on the final kana. The matching た-form endings follow the same rows as って, んで, いて, いで, and して, becoming った, んだ, いた, いだ, and した.

Final Kana (五段) て-Form Suffix
う / つ / る って
む / ぶ / ぬ んで
く いて
ぐ いで
す して

Exception: 行く becomes 行って and 行った.

Notes

  • The ichidan potential form 食べられる has the same surface form as the passive, so context determines which meaning is intended.
  • The same overlap appears with こられる for くる.
  • 食べよ is a valid imperative, but 食べろ is generally more common in modern usage.
  • For godan verbs ending in う, the negative surfaces with わ rather than a visible あ-row kana: 買う -> 買わない.
